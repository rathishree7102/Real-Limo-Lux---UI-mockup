Web Experience

An end-to-end premium ride journey

Designed for a premium ride-booking web application where customers can browse every page — homepage, fleet, and vehicle details — completely freely without an account, and even fill in the Request This Vehicle form on the details page. Login and sign-up are only required when the request is submitted, keeping discovery frictionless and engineering the experience to feel effortless from first visit to ride confirmation.

Step by step

The detailed customer journey

Each stage of the flow chart, expanded with what the customer sees, what the web app captures, and where the journey can branch.

1
Home / Landing — anyone can open the website — no login required. The landing page presents a hero banner, Why Choose Us, How Booking Works, testimonials, and a final CTA. A sticky header keeps the notification and profile icons always one click away.Open to all visitors. Sticky header: notification icon, profile icon.
2
Fleet browsing — explore the luxury fleet freely with search and filters for category and passenger capacity (no luggage filter). Each card surfaces image, category, passenger/luggage capacity, estimated pricing, and a CTA.Open to all visitors. Captures: filter intent, vehicle interest. Branches: refine filters or open a vehicle.
3
Vehicle details — a detail page open to everyone with an image gallery, amenities, and estimated pricing only (no final fare shown on the website). A Request This Vehicle form is embedded right on this page capturing pickup/drop, multi-stop routing, date and time ranges, duration, and special requests. Guests can fill it in freely.Open to all visitors. Captures: full trip parameters as they are entered.
4
Authentication (gate on submit) — login is only required when the customer submits the Request This Vehicle form. If a guest clicks the CTA without being signed in, they are prompted to log in or sign up via Email + OTP, Phone OTP, Social login, or a fresh sign-up; returning users with a remembered session submit without interruption. After verification the entered details are submitted as they were.Triggered by the form CTA. Captures: identity, verified contact, session token. Branches: new vs. returning user.
5
Trip summary — a clear booking summary with estimated fare range, selected vehicle, route details, and total duration shown before final confirmation.Branches: edit trip (back to form) or confirm request.
6
Booking request received — an acknowledgement page with a message confirming the booking request was received.Next actions: Go to Home, Book more rides.
7
Notifications — a central in-page notification center keeps customers updated on booking confirmation, driver assignment, and pre-trip reminders (24 hours and 2 hours before pickup). Push notifications are mandatory; email and SMS are sent only when enabled.Triggers: booking confirmed, driver assigned, 24h reminder, 2h reminder.
8
Profile — users track active bookings, review booking history and rate completed rides (no receipts), manage personal information and communication preferences (email and SMS), and store frequent pickup/drop addresses. No payment management.Modules: Current bookings, History & ratings, Profile settings, Communication preferences, Saved addresses.
9
Contact — customers reach the team directly by phone or email. There is no separate support or enquiries section — just the listed contact details.Channels: phone, email.

Build reference

Page-by-page specifications

Developer-facing detail for each page: access control, key components, primary actions, the data it reads or writes, and the UI states to implement.

1 · Landing / Home

Home page
Public

Sections / components

  • Hero banner with primary Book Now CTA.
  • Why Choose Us (value props).
  • How Booking Works (3–4 step explainer).
  • Testimonials (carousel or grid).
  • Final CTA band.
  • Sticky header: notification icon + profile icon only.

Actions & data

  • Book Now CTA opens the Fleet page.
  • Profile icon opens the Login page for guests, otherwise the Profile page.
  • Reads: marketing content, featured testimonials.

2 · Fleet Listing

Fleet page
Public

Components

  • Search input (vehicle name / keyword).
  • Filters: category, passenger capacity. No price filter and no luggage filter.
  • Vehicle card grid: image, category, name, color, passenger & luggage capacity, estimated pricing, CTA.

Actions & data

  • Clicking a vehicle card opens the Vehicle Details page.
  • Reads: vehicle list, filtered by category, passenger capacity, and search keyword.

3 · Vehicle Details

Vehicle Details page
Public

Components

  • Image gallery / carousel.
  • Vehicle name, category, capacity, amenities list.
  • Estimated pricing only — no final fare shown on the website.
  • Request This Vehicle form embedded on this page: pickup & drop (with saved-address autofill), multi-stop routing (add / remove stops), date & time, duration, special requests.
  • Primary Request This Vehicle CTA.

Actions & data

  • Guests can fill in the form freely. Clicking the CTA requires login if not signed in.
  • On valid submit (signed in): go to Trip Summary with the entered details. Full field spec in the Booking Form section.
  • Reads: the selected vehicle's details; saved addresses for logged-in users.

4 · Login / Sign-up (gate on form submit)

Login · Sign-up · OTP Verification pages
Auth required

Components

  • Login: Email + OTP, Phone + OTP, Social (Google / Apple).
  • OTP verification step (resend timer, channel fallback email ⇄ phone).
  • Sign-up: basic details form + Google / Apple sign-up.

Actions & data

  • Triggered only when a guest submits the Request This Vehicle form; preserves the entered details.
  • Writes: request OTP, verify OTP, social sign-in.
  • On success: create session, then submit the entered details and continue to Trip Summary.

5 · Trip Summary

Trip Summary page
Auth required

Components

  • Selected vehicle, route & stops, date/time, total duration.
  • Estimated fare range (estimate only — not a final charge).
  • Edit and Confirm actions.

Actions & data

  • Edit returns to the Vehicle Details page's Request This Vehicle form (state preserved).
  • Confirm submits the booking request and opens the Booking Request Received page.

6 · Booking Request Received

Booking Request Received page
Auth required

Components

  • Acknowledgement message — request received.
  • Go to Home and Book More Rides CTAs.
  • No booking reference, fare, or next-steps panel on this page.

Actions & data

  • Go to Home opens the Home page; Book More Rides opens the Fleet page.
  • Booking already persisted at confirm step.

Form contract

Request This Vehicle form — fields & validation

Field-level specification for the Request This Vehicle form embedded on the Vehicle Details page. Use this as the validation contract between front-end and API. Required fields block submission until valid. Guests can enter values; submitting requires login.

FieldTypeRequiredValidation / notes
Pickup locationAddress / placeRequiredNon-empty; supports saved-address autofill. Store label + coordinates if available.
Drop locationAddress / placeRequiredNon-empty; must differ from pickup.
Additional stopsRepeatable addressOptional0..n stops; each validated like a location; preserve order.
DateDateRequiredToday or future; reject past dates.
TimeTimeRequiredIf date is today, time must be in the future (respect a lead-time buffer if defined).
DurationNumber / rangeRequiredPositive value; used in the fare estimate.
PassengersNumberOptionalMust not exceed the selected vehicle's passenger capacity.
Special requestsTextOptionalFree text; enforce a max length (e.g. 500 chars).

On submit, the front-end validates all required fields inline before advancing to Trip Summary. The fare shown anywhere is an estimate only, never a final charge.

Requirements

User stories

Each feature expressed as a user story with acceptance criteria, grouped by module. Format: As a [user], I want [goal] so that [benefit]. Use these for sprint planning and QA sign-off.

Authentication

US-01 · Email + OTP login

As a returning customer, I want to log in with my email and a one-time code so that I can access my account without a password.

Acceptance criteria
  • Entering a registered email sends an OTP to that email.
  • A valid OTP creates an authenticated session.
  • Invalid or expired OTP shows a clear error and allows resend.
US-02 · Phone OTP login

As a customer, I want to log in or sign up with my mobile number and an SMS code so that I can authenticate quickly on any device.

Acceptance criteria
  • Entering a valid mobile number sends an SMS OTP.
  • New numbers proceed to sign-up; existing numbers log in.
  • Resend is available after a countdown timer.
US-03 · OTP verification

As a customer, I want a secure OTP verification step so that only I can access my account.

Acceptance criteria
  • OTP expires after a set time and cannot be reused.
  • Fallback between email ⇄ phone channels is offered after failures.
  • Successful verification validates the session.
US-04 · Social login

As a customer, I want to log in with Google or Apple so that I can onboard faster without new credentials.

Acceptance criteria
  • Google and Apple sign-in options are available on login.
  • Email is verified through the provider flow.
  • A session is created on successful authorization.
US-05 · Sign up

As a new visitor, I want to create an account with basic details (or Google / Apple) so that I can book a ride.

Acceptance criteria
  • Sign-up collects basic details and verifies via OTP.
  • Google and Apple sign-up are also available.
  • The session is remembered for return visits.

Browsing & Discovery

US-06 · Open browsing

As a visitor, I want to browse all pages without logging in so that I can explore services before committing.

Acceptance criteria
  • Home, Fleet, and Vehicle Details are accessible without an account.
  • Login is only requested when booking starts.
US-07 · Landing page

As a visitor, I want a landing page with clear value props and how it works so that I understand the service and can start booking.

Acceptance criteria
  • Shows hero, Why Choose Us, How Booking Works, testimonials, final CTA.
  • Primary CTA leads to the fleet.
US-08 · Fleet listing

As a visitor, I want to browse the available vehicles so that I can find one that suits my trip.

Acceptance criteria
  • Vehicles display as cards with image, category, capacity, and estimated pricing.
  • Clicking a card opens its detail page.
US-09 · Search & filters

As a visitor, I want to search and filter vehicles by category and passenger capacity so that I can narrow results quickly.

Acceptance criteria
  • Filters cover category and passenger capacity (no price filter, no luggage filter).
  • No matches shows an empty state with a relaxed-filter hint.
US-10 · Vehicle details

As a visitor, I want to view vehicle photos, amenities, and an estimated price so that I can decide before booking.

Acceptance criteria
  • Detail page shows a gallery, amenities, and estimated pricing only.
  • No final fare is displayed on the website.
  • The Request This Vehicle form is embedded on this page.

Booking

US-11 · Request This Vehicle form

As a visitor, I want to enter my trip details (including multi-stop routing) right on the vehicle details page so that I can request the exact ride I need without leaving the page.

Acceptance criteria
  • Form is embedded on the Vehicle Details page (Request This Vehicle).
  • Captures pickup/drop, additional stops, date, time, duration, special requests.
  • Guests can fill it in; required fields are validated inline.
  • Saved addresses can autofill location fields for logged-in users.
US-12 · Trip summary

As a customer, I want to review a summary with an estimated fare before confirming so that I know what I am requesting.

Acceptance criteria
  • Shows vehicle, route, date/time, duration, and estimated fare range.
  • I can edit (back to the details form) or confirm the request.
US-13 · Login on submit

As a guest, I want to be prompted to log in only when I submit the request so that my browsing and form-filling stay frictionless.

Acceptance criteria
  • Guests can fill the Request This Vehicle form without logging in.
  • Clicking the CTA prompts login/sign-up if not signed in.
  • After auth, the entered details are submitted as they were.
US-14 · Request received

As a customer, I want a clear confirmation that my request was received so that I know it went through.

Acceptance criteria
  • Shows an acknowledgement message after confirm.
  • Provides Go to Home and Book More Rides CTAs.

Handover

Business rules & developer notes

Key constraints to honour during implementation. These define the boundaries of the customer experience.

Open browsing, login on submit

All informational pages — Home, Fleet, and Vehicle Details — are fully public. The Request This Vehicle form lives on the Vehicle Details page and guests can fill it in freely. Authentication is required only when the form is submitted. The login prompt must preserve the entered details and submit them after sign-in.

Estimated pricing only

The website never shows a final fare. Vehicle cards, details, and the trip summary display an estimate only. No price-based filtering on the fleet listing.

No payments in-product

There is no payment management in the user profile and no checkout/payment step in the flow. Booking ends at a request acknowledgement.

Minimal sticky header

The sticky header contains the notification icon and profile icon only — no inline fleet links or Book Now button in the header.

Request acknowledgement, not confirmation

After confirm, the customer sees a simple "request received" message with Go to Home and Book More Rides CTAs — no reference number or next-steps panel.

Contact, not support

Support is limited to a phone number (shown as text, not click-to-call) and an email address. There is no enquiry form, live chat, bulk-booking flow, or ticketing system.

Authentication options

Login via Email OTP, Phone OTP, or Social (Google / Apple). Sign-up collects basic details and also supports Google / Apple. Sessions are remembered for returning users.

Ratings, notifications & preferences

Customers can rate completed rides from Booking History (no receipts). Push notifications are mandatory; email and SMS are optional channels per the user's communication preferences. Reminders fire 24 hours and 2 hours before pickup.

Field limits and data references in this document are suggestions for alignment — confirm against the final backend contract before implementation.
